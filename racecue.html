Factors in Game Design

There are 4 factors which play a role in any game. All games have one or more of these dictating the gameplay. After looking at a variety of games during the process, we realised the combination of Skill and Strategy was relatively uncommon. We hence decided to take it up as a challenge.

Focus Area ‚Äî Disaster Management

We picked the starting point of our game as disaster management and based our ideation around that. We mapped aspects of disaster management to possible game mechanics.

Version 1- Resource Cards + Strategy

The first version was a strategy based game with resource cards and complex rules. It involved a lot of thinking which most kids did not find that much fun. It proved to be too complicated for children.

Version was an earthquake themed game, we tested it with children.

Simplifying it to the current game

We then constrained our focus only on the concept of rescuing. This involved compromising most of the complex educational aspects of the game but in the end, fun comes first in a game for kids. We added physical skill to make it more engaging.

Board, Setup and Pieces‚Ä¶

The board is a two level board, the pieces of all the players are placed on the lower level. The upper level is a hexagonal grid which is placed over it. The grid holds hexagonal tiles which are used to play the game.

There are three types of tiles. These are moved and replaced by other tiles on the grid. During the beginning, every player starts with two small clearings in their hand to be used on the grid.

The helicopter sticks are used to lift pieces off the board through the clearings.

CYCLE OF PLAY üîÑ

This is a 3 Player game. There are two types of moves LIFT/REPLACE and SWAP. If one player does one type of move then the next player has to do the other kind. This was the moves alternate with each cycle for all players.

The alternate moves in each cycle compel players to think ahead and form strategy.

TYPES OF MOVES

Type 1- LIFT / REPLACE üöÅ

In this type of move a player has the choice between lifting and replacing.

LIFT- Rescuing your pieces
If you choose to lift, you can lift a piece off the lower level only if the tile above it has a clearing. It is easy to lift a piece through a large clearing and hard to lift it through a small clearing.

You have 10 seconds to lift a piece.

Loudly chant ‚Äú1 Rescue, 2 Rescue, 3 Rescue, 4 Rescue, 5 Rescue....10 Rescue üéµ‚Äù as a timer while a player tries to lift, for fun and to build pressure.

Case 1- Reward for Successful Lift

When you successfully lift a piece there is a reward, you get a tile with a large clearing to use.

Successful rescue by Red.

Case 2- Penalty for Unsuccessful Lift

If you fail to rescue a piece in 10 seconds, you get a penalty, the clearing you used gets replaced by a blocked tile.

Unsuccessful rescue by Red.
......the game rewards a steady hand.

REPLACE - Creating new opportunities for rescue

If you choose to replace, you replace one of the tiles on the grid with one in your hand (see booklet for rules).

Replacing tiles opens up new opportunities for rescue in the game.
Red creates a rescue opportunity by putting clearing over his piece.

Type 2- SWAP

In this type of move you swap positions of adjacent pieces. This can be used to steal rescue opportunities from other players and put yourself in advantageous positions.

Swapping tiles is the main strategic move in the game.
